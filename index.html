MICHAEL A. KATELL FELLOWSHIP

The Michael A. Katell Fellowship was established to honor and extend the memory of Dr. Michael A. Katell (1965-2024), co-founder and director of the Critical Platform Studies Group (CritPlat) and Ethics Fellow at The Alan Turing Institute. The fellowship aims to assist students who are collaborating with community-based organizations to pursue projects for social, racial, or environmental justice in information science, computer science, or a related department.

Criteria:

  • An interest in the use of research to advance justice and equity;
  • A commitment to community-driven work addressing real-world problems;
  • Warmth, kindness, and a commitment to living your values.

Organizing committee:

This fellowship is organized by the following people to collect and disburse funds, draft the fellowship application, invite others to help review and select a fellow, and plan for the continuation of the fellowship for future years.

  • Meg Young, Data & Society Research Institute
  • Peaks Krafft, University of Edinburgh
  • David Leslie, Alan Turing Institute

The fellowship is accepting donations through 2024 and will open for fellowship applications on January 1. The disbursement will be made in lump sum, the amount will be determined by fundraising, number of fellows, and planning over time.
